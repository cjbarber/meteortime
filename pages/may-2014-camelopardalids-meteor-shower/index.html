Camelopardalids Meteor Shower - Bay Area Viewing Guide (Concise)

Also Known As: Comet 209P/LINEAR

Peak: Friday, May 23rd 2014 (SOON!)

General Tips

  • Estimated quantity: 100-400 meteors per hour
  • Potentially the best meteor shower of the year (it's a new meteor shower, so we don't know for sure yet)
  • West Coast Best Viewing Time: 11pm to 1am (on the night of Friday the 23rd, so the early morning of Saturday the 24th of May)
  • East Coast Best Viewing Time: 2am to 4am (on the night of Friday the 23rd, so the early morning of Saturday the 24th of May)
  • Moon status: small! (This is great)
  • Best hemisphere: Northern. Far better in USA.
  • Don't use a telescope.
  • Note: _"Because this is a new meteor shower, surprises are possible. Outbursts could occur hours before or after the forecasted peak."_

Bay Area Viewing

Note, that this meteor shower is viewed by looking North. Therefore, these locations are specific to the Camelopardalid Meteor Shower, and won't necessarily apply to others.

Unfortunately, with San Francisco being so bright, the general rule is that people in the city will need to head north, and people in the South Bay will need to head south/south-east.
